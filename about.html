Founder

Marvin Mmembe Ngabonzira

I am a third-year Mechatronics and Biomedical Engineering student at Kyambogo University, Kampala, Uganda — working at the intersection of brain-computer interfaces, assistive technology, and the question of what it means to do rigorous science with limited resources.

The Science Blueprint exists because I could not find science communication that spoke to people building in contexts like mine — where lab equipment is scarce, internet is patchy, and the nearest expert is on the other side of the world. So I started building it myself.

Current research: A-MDRM (Adaptive Minimum Distance to Riemannian Mean) — a BCI classifier for motor-imagery EEG signals under pseudo-realistic noisy conditions, using Riemannian geometry. Final Year Project, Kyambogo University.
Supervisors: Mr. Bagaiga Richard (BCI research) · Dr. Kafeero Hussein (Assistive Technology, Kyambogo University)
Target (2028): Global Korea Scholarship — Master's in Neuroengineering, South Korea. Language acquisition in progress: Korean (TOPIK II Level 4 target).

The Lucid Standard

How I Work

A set of working principles formalized as The Lucid Standard v1.0. Twelve commitments I hold myself to in research, communication, and building.

01
Publish everything. Withholding knowledge is waste.
02
Open access is not charity. It is the correct default.
03
Constraints are not excuses. They are parameters to engineer around.
04
Clarity is a form of respect for the reader.
05
Build in public. The work is not finished when it is done — it is finished when it is understood.
06
Context is not an afterthought. African science needs African framing.
07
Intellectual honesty over comfort, every time.
08
The standard is the blueprint. Not the result — the methodology.
